AMENDME NT 1257D
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: Representative Soye
1 An Act to regulate the performance and reporting of abortions necessary to preserve
2 the life of a pregnant female and to provide a penalty therefor amend the
3 definition of an abortion.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
5
6 Section 1. That chapter 22-17 be amended with a NEW SECTION:
7 Terms used in this chapter mean:
8 (1) "Abortion," the act of administering, prescribing, providing, selling, or using any drug,
9 medicine, or other substance, or providing, selling, or using any device or instrument, with
10 the intent to terminate a clinically diagnosable pregnancy, including the elimination of one or
11 more unborn children in a multifetal pregnancy, with knowledge that the termination by those
12 means will, with reasonable likelihood, cause the death of the unborn child, provided the term
13 does not include:
14 (a) Medical treatment that is provided to a pregnant female and results in the accidental or
15 unintentional death of the unborn child;
16 (b) Treatment to resolve a miscarriage;
17 (c) The treatment or removal of an ectopic pregnancy;
18 (d) The removal from the uterus of a deceased unborn child; or
19 (e) Any medical procedure performed for the purpose of saving the life or preserving the
20 health of the unborn child;
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.
1257D 2 1257
1 (2) "Ectopic pregnancy," the implantation of an embryo outside the uterus, in an abnormal
2 location in the uterus, or in a scarred portion of the uterus;
3 (3) "Miscarriage," the spontaneous loss of a pregnancy, including the circumstances of an
4 inevitable miscarriage, an incomplete miscarriage, a missed miscarriage, and a septic
5 miscarriage;
6 (4) "Pregnant", the human female reproductive condition of having a living unborn child within
7 the female's body, throughout every stage of the unborn child's life and development, from
8 fertilization to full gestation and childbirth;
9 (5) "Reasonable medical judgment," a medical judgment that would be made by a reasonably
10 prudent physician, knowledgeable about the case and the treatment possibilities with respect
11 to the medical condition involved; and
12 (6) "Unborn child," an individual organism of the species homo sapiens from fertilization until
13 live birth.
14 Section 1. That § 22-17-5.1 be AMENDED:
15 22-17-5.1. Any person who administers to any pregnant female or who prescribes
16 or procures for any pregnant female any medicine, drug, or substance or uses or employs
17 any instrument or other means with intent thereby to procure commits an abortion is
18 guilty of a Class 6 felony, as defined in § 34-23A-1, unless there is appropriate and in the
19 reasonable medical judgment that performance of an attending physician an abortion is
20 necessary to preserve the life of the pregnant female, is guilty of a Class 6 felony.
21 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 22-17:
22 For an abortion to be justified as necessary to preserve the life of a pregnant
23 female, as set forth in § 22-17-5.1, the attending physician, exercising reasonable medical
24 judgment, must determine that the female suffers from a physical disorder, physical
25 illness, or physical injury, including a life-endangering physical condition arising from the
26 pregnancy itself, and that the disorder, illness, or injury places the pregnant female at a
27 reasonably foreseeable risk of death, unless an abortion is performed. Nothing in this
28 section requires that the risk of death be imminent.
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.
1257D 3 1257
1 The attending physician, exercising reasonable medical judgement, shall make
2 reasonable efforts, under the circumstances, to preserve both the life of the pregnant
3 female and the life of her unborn child, unless in reasonable medical judgment, the
4 termination of the pregnancy in that manner would pose a greater risk of death of the
5 pregnant female.
6 An abortion justified under this section must take place at a hospital licensed
7 pursuant to chapter 34-12, unless, due to a medical emergency, it is necessary to perform
8 the abortion in another location. The person performing or inducing the abortion must be
9 a licensed physician.
10 The physician performing or inducing the abortion shall report the abortion to the
11 Department of Health in accordance with the requirements set forth in chapter 34-23A.
12 An abortion may not be construed as necessary to preserve the life of the pregnant
13 female if:
14 (1) The abortion is based on the physician's belief that the female may or will take
15 action to harm herself; or
16 (2) The abortion is based on a claim or diagnosis that relates to a psychological or
17 emotional condition of the pregnant female.
18 Section 3. That § 22-17-6 be AMENDED:
19 22-17-6. Any person who intentionally kills a human fetus an unborn child by
20 causing an injury to its the child's mother, which is not authorized by chapter 34-23A, is
21 guilty of a Class 4 felony.
22 Section 4. That § 22-17-13 be AMENDED:
23 22-17-13. A person is guilty of a Class B felony if, with the intent to cause a
24 pregnant mother to undergo an abortion against her will, the person:
25 (1) Threatens With the intent to cause a pregnant mother to undergo an abortion
26 against her will, the person threatens to commit, against the pregnant mother or
27 any other person within the pregnant mother's presence:
28 (a) Homicide, murder, or manslaughter, under chapter 22-16;
Underscores indicate new language.

1 (b) Aggravated assault, under § 22-18-1.1; or
2 (c) Kidnapping, under chapter 22-19; and
3 (2) The threat, as described in subdivision (1) of this section, results in the death of
4 the unborn human being, as defined under § 34-23A-1 child.
5 A charge brought under this section may be commenced at any time prior to the
6 time the victim attains age twenty-five or within seven years of the commission of the
7 crime, whichever is longer.

27 fertilization until live birth.

29 A physician who performs or induces an abortion for the purpose of preserving the
30 life of a pregnant female, as set forth in § 22-17-5.1 and section 3 of this Act, shall prepare
31 a document:
32 (1) Attesting that the abortion was necessitated by the pregnant female's medical
33 condition;
