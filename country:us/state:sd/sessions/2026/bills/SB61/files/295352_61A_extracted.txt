Section 1:
61A 101st Legislative Session 61
2026 South Dakota Legislature
AMENDM ENT 61A
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: Senator Carley
1 An Act to ban hemp-derived intoxicants not for medical purposes.
2 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
3 Section 1. That § 34-20B-118 be AMENDED:
4 34-20B-118. No Except as otherwise provided in this section, no person or entity
5 may:
6 (1) Chemically modify or convert industrial hemp as defined in § 38-35-1, or engage
7 in any process that converts cannabidiol, into delta-8:
8 (a) Delta-8 tetrahydrocannabinol, delta-9;
9 (b) Delta-9 tetrahydrocannabinol, delta-10;
10 (c) Delta-10 tetrahydrocannabinol, or any; or
11 (d) Any other tetrahydrocannabinol isomer, analog, or derivative; or
12 (2) Sell or distribute industrial hemp or an industrial hemp product that contains
13 chemically derived cannabinoids or cannabinoids created by chemically modifying
14 or converting a hemp extract.; or
15 (3) Sell, distribute, possess, manufacture, or consume an industrial hemp product
16 intended for human or animal consumption, or any intermediate hemp-derived
17 cannabinoid products containing cannabinoids that:
18 (a) Are not capable of being naturally produced by a cannabis sativa plant;
19 (b) Are capable of being naturally produced by a cannabis sativa plant but were
20 synthesized or manufactured outside the plant; or
21 (c) Are in amounts greater than 0.4 milligrams combined total of any
22 tetrahydrocannabinol isomer, analog, or derivative, per container.
23 This section does not apply to medical cannabis or medical cannabis products
24 regulated by the Department of Health under chapter 34-20G.
25 A violation of this section is a Class 2 misdemeanor.
26 Section 2. That § 38-35-1 be AMENDED:

1 38-35-1. Terms used in this chapter mean:
2 (1) "Applicant," a person, including the state or any agency or institution thereof, any
3 municipality, political subdivision, public or private corporation, individual,
4 partnership, limited liability company, association, or trust; and includes any officer
5 or governing or managing body of any municipality, political subdivision, or public
6 or private corporation, or limited liability company, applying for an industrial hemp
7 grower license, processor license, or both;
8 (2) "Department," the Department of Agriculture and Natural Resources;
9 (3)(2) "Chemically derived cannabinoid," a chemical substance created by a chemical
10 reaction that changes the molecular structure of any chemical substance derived
11 from the cannabis plant. The term does not include:
12 (a) Cannabinoids produced by decarboxylation from a naturally occurring
13 cannabinoid acid without the use of a chemical catalyst;
14 (b) Non-psychoactive cannabinoids; or
15 (c)(b) Cannabinoids in a topical cream product;
16 (3) "Department," the Department of Agriculture and Natural Resources;
17 (4) "Greenhouse," any indoor structure or enclosed building capable of being used for
18 continuous cultivation throughout the year, no less than two thousand eight
19 hundred and eighty square feet, and not part of a residential dwelling. Greenhouses
20 may contain multiple lots that are separated and identified;
21 (5) "Hemp" or "industrial hemp," the plant Cannabis sativa L., whether growing or not,
22 and any part of that plant, including the seeds thereof and all derivatives, extracts,
23 cannabinoids, isomers, acids, salts, and salts of isomers, whether growing or not,
24 with a total delta-9 tetrahydrocannabinol concentration of not more than three-
25 tenths of one percent on a dry weight basis;
26 (6) "Key participant," a sole proprietor, a partner in a partnership, a principal executive
27 officer for a government entity, or a person with executive managerial control in a
28 corporation or limited liability company;
29 (7)(6) "Industrial hemp product," a finished manufactured product, or consumer product
30 made from industrial hemp with a total delta-9 tetrahydrocannabinol concentration
31 of not more than three-tenths of one percent, derived from or made by processing
32 industrial hemp;. This term does not include a product containing chemically
33 derived cannabinoids, including:
34 (a) Delta-8 tetrahydrocannabinol, also known as delta-8-THC;
35 (b) Delta-10 tetrahydrocannabinol, also known as delta-10-THC;
