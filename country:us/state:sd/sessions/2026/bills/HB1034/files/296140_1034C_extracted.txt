1 An Act to increase fees for decals and license plates mailed to vehicle owners.

3 Section 1. That § 32-3A-5.2 be AMENDED:
4 32-3A-5.2. In addition to the registration fee required under § 32-3A-5, the
5 department or county treasurer shall collect from charge the owner one dollar and fifty
6 two dollars and fifty cents per decal or set of decals if a decal is sent to the owner through
7 the mail. If the applicant requests that a decal be express mailed, the applicant shall pay
8 the actual cost of postage and handling. The county treasurer shall deposit any fees for
9 mailing or expressing decals in the county general fund.

11 32-5-127. In addition to the registration fees required by this chapter, the
12 department or county treasurer shall collect from charge the owner one dollar and fifty,

14 (1) Two dollars and fifty cents per decal or set of decals; and seven dollars and fifty
15 cents
16 (2) Twelve dollars per license plate or set of plates when plates are sent to the owner
17 through the mail.

21 dealer one dollar and fifty, when sent to the owner through the mail:

23 cents; and

25 32-9-3.1. Any motor vehicle or trailer owned and operated by a resident or a

1 Each permit, which is valid for a calendar year, must be purchased from the county
2 treasurer of any county through which the owner or operator may travel, or from an agent,
3 patrol officer, motor carrier enforcement officer, or motor carrier inspector of the
4 Department of Public Safety. If the applicant requests that the permit be mailed, the
5 applicant must pay one dollar two dollars and fifty cents per permit sent to the owner
6 through the mail. All fees collected shall be handled, accounted for, and distributed in the
7 same manner as the other fees provided for in this chapter. A violation of this section is a
8 Class 2 misdemeanor.
9 Section 8. That § 32-9-7 be AMENDED:
10 32-9-7. On receipt of an application under § 32-9-6 and payment of the
11 commercial motor vehicle fee, required by this chapter, and upon satisfactory evidence
12 that the applicant has complied with all laws, rules, and regulations of this state covering
13 motor vehicles and motor carriers, the county treasurer or department shall issue to the
14 applicant a receipt that identifies the motor vehicle, trailer, or semitrailer and shall assign
15 a number to the vehicle. The number shall be endorsed upon the application and receipt.
16 The county treasurer or department shall issue to the applicant a commercial motor
17 vehicle certificate bearing the number. The certificate shall be placed and carried in the
18 vehicle in a conspicuous place and is subject to examination upon demand by any officer
19 of this state, county, or municipality. The county treasurer or department shall issue to
20 the applicant two commercial motor vehicle plates for each motor vehicle. Each county
21 treasurer office shall be stocked with commercial license plates issued pursuant to this
22 section and commercial trailer license plates issued pursuant to § 32-9-8.1.
23 If the plates are mailed, the applicant must pay seven twelve dollars and fifty cents
24 per license plate or set of plates if the plate is sent to the owner through the mail, or one
25 dollar two dollars and fifty cents per decal or set of decals if the decal is sent to the owner
26 through the mail. If the applicant requests that the plate or decal be express mailed, the
27 applicant must pay any costs for the express mailing service.
28 Each plate must set forth the amount of gross weight in figures, and be in colors
29 and designs for each classification specified in § 32-9-15. Each plate must be securely
30 fastened to the front and rear end of each commercial motor vehicle in a conspicuous
31 place.
32 The county treasurer shall deposit in the county general fund any fees received for
33 mailing or expressing a plate or sticker. The department shall deposit all fees received for

1 mailing or expressing the plates or stickers in the state motor vehicle fund. A violation of
2 this section is a Class 2 misdemeanor.
3 Section 9. That § 32-10-17 be AMENDED:
4 32-10-17. The administrator shall register the vehicles described and identified in
5 an application pursuant to § 32-10-15 and shall issue a license plate or plates, or a vehicle
6 registration card, or other suitable identification device, for each vehicle described in the
7 application upon payment of the fees for registration and licensing and for the vehicle
8 registration cards or other identification devices. A fee of three dollars must be paid for
9 each card or device issued for each proportionally registered vehicle. The card must, in
10 addition to the information required by chapter 32-5, identify the number of the license
11 or other device issued for the proportionally registered vehicle and must be carried in the
12 vehicle at all times or, in the case of a combination, in the vehicle supplying the motive
13 power. For purposes of this section, the operator may provide proof in either paper or
14 electronic format including a display of an electronic image on an electronic device.
15 In addition to the registration fees, the department shall collect from charge the
16 owner seven dollars and fifty cents, when sent to the owner through the mail:
17 (1) Twelve dollars per license plate or set of plates when a plate is sent to the owner
18 through the mail, or one dollar; and
19 (2) Two dollars and fifty cents per decal or set of decals if a decal is sent to the owner
20 through the mail.
21 If the applicant requests that the plate or decal be express mailed, the applicant
22 must pay any costs for the express mailing service.
