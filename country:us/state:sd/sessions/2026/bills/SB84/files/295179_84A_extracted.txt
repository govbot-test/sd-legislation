AMENDM ENT 84A
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: Senator Peterson (Sue)
1 An Act to increase thresholds for the value of and eligibility for the partners in
2 education scholarship.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That § 13-65-1 be AMENDED:
5 13-65-1. Terms, as used in this chapter, mean:
6 (1) "Certified enrollment," the K-12 enrollment data required to be submitted to the
7 Department of Education by an accredited school by October fifteenth of each year
8 and published by the department on the department's website;
9 (2) "Division," the Division of Insurance in the Department of Labor and Regulation;
10 (3) "Educational scholarship," a grant to an eligible student to cover all or part of the
11 tuition and fees at a qualifying school. The average value of all scholarships
12 awarded by a scholarship granting organization may not exceed eighty-two and
13 five-tenths percent of the state's share of the per student equivalent, as defined in
14 § 13-13-10.1;
15 (4) "Eligible student," any student entering kindergarten through twelfth grade who
16 resides in South Dakota while receiving the educational scholarship and:
17 (a) Is a member of a household whose total annual income, the year before the
18 student enters the program, did not exceed one hundred fifty two hundred
19 percent of the income standard used to qualify for a free or reduced-price
20 lunch under the national free or reduced-price lunch program. If sufficient
21 funding is available, once Once a student meets the initial income eligibility
22 requirement, the student remains income eligible for three years or if the
23 student is entering high school, until the student graduates high school
24 regardless of household income. After the initial period of income eligibility,
25 a student remains eligible if the student is a member of a to reapply for and
26 receive an educational scholarship in subsequent years, if the student's
