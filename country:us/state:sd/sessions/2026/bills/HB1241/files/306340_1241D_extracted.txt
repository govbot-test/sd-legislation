Section 1:
1241D 101st Legislative Session 1241
2026 South Dakota Legislature
AMENDME NT 1241D
FOR THE SENATE TAXATION ENGROSSED BILL
Introduced by: Representative Jensen (Phil)
1 An Act to modify the requirements for public notice of a hearing prior to a vote to
2 impose an excess tax levy, and to modify requirements to refer an excess tax
3 levy of a school district to a vote.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
5 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 10-12:
6 At least twenty-one days prior to a vote by the board of a school district to impose
7 an excess tax levy pursuant to § 10-12-43 or 10-12-43.1, the board shall publish, in the
8 legal newspaper designated pursuant to § 13-8-10 and on the district’s website, notice of
9 its intent to vote on the imposition of an excess tax levy.
10 The county director of equalization treasurer shall mail or electronically transmit
11 the notice to all property owners in the district within the county's boundaries, and the
12 school district shall reimburse the county for printing and all mailing costs, including the
13 printing costs and the time required to mail notices, incurred by the county pursuant to
14 this section.
15 The notice must include:
16 (1) The maximum amount under consideration in excess of the limitations applicable
17 to the school district;
18 (2) The amount allowed to be imposed without an excess tax levy;
19 (3) The date, time, and location of the public hearing at which the board will vote to
20 impose an excess tax levy;
21 (4) The estimated increase in property taxes, in the district per one hundred thousand
22 dollars of taxable value, as a result of the excess tax levy; and
23 (5) The intended use of moneys collected from the excess tax levy being considered.
24 The board of the school district shall, at the public hearing, provide taxpayers with
25 an opportunity to comment on the excess tax levy.

1 For purposes of this section, the maximum cost of the time required to mail notices
2 may not exceed ten cents per mailed notice.
3 Section 2. That § 10-12-43 be AMENDED:
4 10-12-43. The governing body of the school district may raise additional revenues
5 for general fund purposes only, from property tax through the imposition of an excess tax
6 levy. The governing body of a school district may impose the excess tax levy with an
7 affirmative two-thirds vote of the governing body on or before July fifteenth of the year
8 prior to the year the taxes are payable. On any excess tax levy approved after July 1,
9 2002, the governing body of the taxing district shall specify in the resolution the year or
10 number of years the excess tax levy will be applied and the total maximum dollar amount
11 of taxes payable over the full term of the excess tax levy.
12 The requirements for an announcement made pursuant to this section are as
13 follows:
14 (1) The decision of the governing body to originally impose or subsequently
15 increase an excess tax levy shall be first published within ten days of the decision;
16 (2) Publication shall be made at least twice in the legal newspaper designated
17 pursuant to § 13-8-10, with no fewer than five days between publication dates, before the
18 opt out takes effect;
19 (3) The announcement shall be at least three newspaper columns in width and four
20 inches in length or at least one-sixth of a page in size, whichever size is greater;
21 (4) The announcement shall be headed with the following statement in a typeface
22 no less than eighteen point type: "ATTENTION TAXPAYERS: NOTICE OF PROPERTY TAX
23 INCREASE OF $(fill in amount)." The remainder of the announcement shall consist of a
24 reproduction of the "Resolution for Opt Out," including the amount that property taxes will
25 be increased annually by the proposed opt out and, the number of years that the excess
26 tax levy is to be applied, and the total maximum dollar amount of taxes payable over the
27 full term of the excess tax levy, a statement of the right to refer the decision of the board
28 to a vote of the people as provided in this section. The secretary of revenue, in rules
29 promulgated pursuant to chapter 1-26, shall prescribe a uniform form to be used by the
30 school district for notification of taxpayers as required by this section.
31 However, the requirements of subdivisions (3) and (4) shall be waived if:
32 (A) The opt out is for less than fifteen thousand dollars; or

1241D 3 1241
1 (B) A copy of the resolution for opt out is mailed to every property taxpayer in the
2 local governmental unit, by first class mail or bulk mail, within twenty days of the decision
3 to opt out; and
4 (C) A copy of the resolution for opt out is printed in each official newspaper in the
5 local governmental unit's boundaries.
6 For the purposes of subsections (A), (B), and (C), the first publication is not
7 deemed to have occurred until three days after the mailing is sent or the resolution is
8 delivered to the official newspaper.
9 The opt out decision may be referred to a vote of the people upon a resolution of
10 the governing body of the school district or by a petition signed by at least five percent of
11 the registered voters in the school district and filed with the governing body within twenty
12 days of the first publication of the decision. The referendum election shall be held on or
13 before October first of the year prior to the time the taxes are payable.
14 Section 3. That § 10-12-43.1 be AMENDED:
15 10-12-43.1. A school district may raise additional revenues for capital outlay fund
16 purposes through the imposition of an excess tax levy. A school district seeking to impose
17 an excess tax levy pursuant to this section is subject to the same opt out procedures and
18 requirements as provided in § 10-12-43. The opt out decision may be referred to a vote
19 of the people in the same manner as provided in § 10-12-43.
20 A school district imposing an excess tax levy pursuant to this section shall exclude
21 any additional revenue generated by the excess tax levy from the total tax revenues
22 deposited in the capital outlay fund when calculating the maximum allowable transfer to
23 the school district's general fund authorized under § 13-16-6. Any additional Additional
24 revenue generated by the excess tax levy may only be used for capital outlay fund
25 purposes pursuant to § 13-16-6.
26 In no year may the annual tax levy for capital outlay fund purposes exceed the
27 levy authorized under § 13-16-7.
28 Section 4. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 10-12:
29 The registered voters of a school district may refer a resolution adopted by the
30 board of a school district to impose an excess tax levy, pursuant to § 10-12-43 or 10-12-
31 43.1, by filing a petition with the business manager of the school district. The petition
32 must be signed by at least five percent of the registered voters of the school district, based
33 on the total number of registered voters of the school district, or signed by two thousand

5 payable over the full term of the excess tax levy. The election must be conducted in the

7 referendum election must be held on or before October first of the year prior to the year
