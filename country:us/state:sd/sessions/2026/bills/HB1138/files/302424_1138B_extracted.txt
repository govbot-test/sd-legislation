1138B 101st Legislative Session 1138
2026 South Dakota Legislature
AMENDME NT 1138B
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: Representative Heermann
1 An Act to require the licensure of non-medical home care agencies, and to provide
2 a penalty therefor.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 34:
5 Terms defined in this chapter mean:
6 (1) "Client," an individual who receives non-medical home care services from a home
7 care aide;
8 (2) "Department," the Department of Health;
9 (3) "Home care aide," an individual employed by or under contract with a non-medical
10 home care agency to provide non-medical home care services;
11 (4) "Non-medical home care agency," a person that, for consideration as a regular part
12 of a business, provides, or employs or contracts with home care aides to provide,
13 non-medical home care services intended to enable a client to remain safely in the
14 client's personal residence; and
15 (5) "Non-medical home care services," the following, when provided by a home care
16 aide to a client in the client's personal residence:
17 (a) Assistance with bathing, dressing, eating, personal grooming, or toileting;
18 (b) Assistance with the self-administration of medications;
19 (c) Companionship;
20 (d) Housekeeping or assistance with household chores;
21 (e) Meal preparation; or
22 (f) Other routine activities of daily living that are not required to be performed
23 by a licensed health care professional.
24 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 34:
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.
1138B 2 1138
1 A non-medical home care agency may not operate in this state unless the agency
2 is licensed by the department.
3 Any person who operates, claims to operate, or advertises as operating, a non-
4 medical home care agency without a license is guilty of a Class 1 misdemeanor.
5 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 34:
6 To obtain or renew a license to operate a non-medical home care agency, a person
7 shall submit to the department:
8 (1) An application, developed by the department;
9 (2) A fee, established by the department in rules promulgated in accordance with
10 chapter 1-26, but not exceeding one hundred dollars;
11 (3) The name and contact information of the agency's owner; and
12 (4) An attestation that:
13 (a) Any home care aides employed by or under contract with the agency have
14 completed training in accordance with section 4 of this Act; and
15 (b) The owner of the agency and any home care aides employed by or under
16 contract with the agency have submitted to and passed a criminal history
17 background check, in accordance with section 5 of this Act.
18 A license issued in accordance with this section is valid for one year from the date
19 of issuance.
20 Any fee submitted under this section is nonrefundable.
21 Section 4. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 34:
22 A non-medical home care agency must ensure that any home care aide employed
23 by or under contract with the agency has completed at least ten hours of training on the
24 provision of non-medical home care services. The training must comprise of:
25 (1) At least two hours on basic care for individuals living with Alzheimer's and
26 dementia; and
27 (2) At least eight hours on:
28 (a) The maintenance of a clean and safe environment;
29 (b) Safe techniques for assisting with bathing, dressing, personal grooming,
30 and toileting;
31 (c) Basic nutrition, meal preparation, food safety, and eating assistance;
32 (d) Client confidentiality, privacy, and documentation and record requirements;
33 and
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.
1138B 3 1138
1 (e) Elder abuse, neglect, and exploitation reporting and prevention.
2 The agency shall maintain, for each home care aide, documentation of the
3 completion of the training, and shall provide the documentation to the department, upon
4 request.
5 A home care aide who is licensed, certified, or registered as a health care
6 professional, in accordance with title 36, satisfies the training requirements of this section
7 if the professional provides documentation to the agency demonstrating training in the
8 topics set forth above, as part of the process for obtaining or renewing licensure,
9 certification, or registration.
10 Section 5. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 34:
11 Before employing or contracting with a home care aide, the non-medical home care
12 agency shall require the prospective employee or contractor to submit to a criminal history
13 background check in accordance with procedures established by the department.
14 Neither the owner of an agency, nor any home care aides employed by or
15 contracted with the agency, may have been convicted of, or pled guilty or nolo contendere
16 to, the following crimes in this state or a comparable crime in another state:
17 (1) A crime of violence, as defined by § 22-1-2;
18 (2) A sex crime listed in § 22-24B-1, or another crime under chapters 22-22, 22-22A,
19 or 22-24A; or
20 (3) A Class A or B felony.
21 The agency may not disseminate any information obtained under this section to a
22 person not employed by the agency.
23 Section 6. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 34:
24 A non-medical home care agency shall maintain records documenting the type and
25 frequency of services provided to each client and shall provide client records to the
26 department upon request.
27 Client records and any personally identifiable information regarding a client are
28 confidential and may be disclosed only as permitted by law or as authorized by the client
29 or the client's designee.
30 Section 7. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 34:
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.
1138B 4 1138
1 The department may inspect a non-medical home care agency, during business
2 hours, for compliance with this chapter. The department may audit an agency.
3 The department shall investigate any complaint alleging a violation of this chapter
4 or rules promulgated thereunder in the same manner as chapter 36-1C.
5 Section 8. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 34:
6 The department may, in accordance with chapter 1-26, deny the issuance or
7 renewal of, or may suspend or revoke, a license issued under this chapter, for a violation
8 of any provision of this chapter or rules promulgated thereunder.
9 Any party aggrieved by any act, ruling, or decision of the department may appeal
10 the act, ruling, or decision under the provisions of chapter 1-26.
11 Section 9. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 34:
12 The department shall promulgate rules, in accordance with chapter 1-26, to
13 establish:
14 (1) The application process for licensure as a non-medical home care agency;
15 (2) Home care aide training program standards necessary to protect the health and
16 safety of clients;
17 (3) Record keeping requirements for agencies; and
18 (4) Procedures for the completion of criminal history background checks for
19 prospective agency employees or contractors.
20 Section 10. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 34:
21 There is created in the state treasury the non-medical home care agency regulatory
22 fund. The fund consists of all fees imposed pursuant to this chapter.
23 The department shall administer the fund. The purpose of the fund is to defray
24 expenses associated with administering this chapter. Interest on moneys credited to the
25 fund must be deposited into the fund. Expenditures from the fund must be budgeted
26 through the general appropriation bill.
27 Section 11. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 34:
28 This chapter does not apply to:
29 (1) A home health agency certified by the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services;
30 (2) A health care facility licensed in accordance with chapter 34-12;
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.
1138B 5 1138
1 (3) A residential care facility registered in accordance with chapter 34-12;
2 (4) A community services provider or a community support provider;
3 (5) Any person who provides services under a medicaid waiver program administered
4 by the Department of Human Services;
5 (6) A health care professional, licensed in accordance with title 36, who provides non-
6 medical home cares services in conjunction with health care services in an
7 individual's personal residence;
8 (7) A person who coordinates, refers, or matches a non-medical home care agency to
9 a potential client, but who does not employ or contract with any home care aides;
10 or
11 (8) An individual who provides care to another family member.
12 Section 12. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 34:
13 Nothing in this chapter authorizes a non-medical home care agency to provide or
14 coordinate services that are required to be provided by a licensed health care professional.
15 Section 13. This Act is effective beginning January 1, 2027.
