1 An Act to revise provisions a provision related to service of process for counties and
2 boards of county commissioners.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That § 7-8-29 be AMENDED:
5 7-8-29. Such appeal shall be taken within twenty days after the publication of the
6 decision of the board by serving a written notice on one of the members of the board, as
7 set forth in subdivision 15-6-4(d)(2)(i) upon any county administrator, commissioner,
8 executive, treasurer, or any registered agent of the board when the appeal is taken by
9 any person aggrieved by the decision of the board, and upon the person or persons
10 affected by the decision of the board when the appeal is taken by the state's attorney;
11 and the county auditor shall upon.
12 Upon the filing of the required bond and the payment of his fees, which shall be
13 the same as allowed registers are equal to those imposed by a register of deeds for like
14 services, the county auditor shall make out a complete transcript of the proceedings of
15 the board relating to the matter of its the board's decision and deliver the same transcript
16 to the clerk of courts.
17 Section 2. That § 15-6-4(d) be AMENDED:
18 15-6-4(d). The summons shall be served by delivering a copy thereof. Service in
19 the following manner shall constitute personal service:
20 (1) If the action is against a business entity, on the president, partner or other head
21 of the entity, officer, director, or registered agent thereof. If any of the above
22 cannot be conveniently found, service may be made by leaving a copy of the
23 summons and complaint at any office of such business entity within this state, with
24 the person in charge of such office;

1 (i) A business entity for purposes of this subdivision shall include, but not be
2 limited to:
3 A. Domestic and foreign corporations;
4 B. Domestic and foreign partnerships;
5 C. Limited liability companies;
6 D. Entities with fictitious names; and
7 E. Any entity required to have a registered agent with the Secretary of
8 State.
9 (2) If the action is against a public corporation within this state, service may be made
10 as follows:
11 (i) Upon a county, by serving upon any county administrator, auditor,
12 commissioner, executive, or any registered agent of the county or board of
13 county commissioners. Any service made pursuant to this section
14 constitutes service upon the county and the board of county commissioners;
15 (ii) Upon a first or second class municipality, by serving upon the mayor or any
16 alderman or commissioner;
17 (iii) Upon a third class municipality, by serving upon any trustee;
18 (iv) Upon an organized township, by serving upon any supervisor;
19 (v) Upon any school district, by serving upon any member of the school board
20 or board of education; and
21 (vi) Upon a consumers power district, by serving upon any member of the board
22 of directors;
23 (3) If the action is against a minor, upon a parent or person having custody, and if the
24 minor is over the age of fourteen years, then also upon such minor personally, and
25 in any event upon the legally appointed guardian, guardian ad litem or conservator,
26 if there is one;
27 (4) If the action is against a person judicially declared to be mentally incompetent, or
28 who is a patient at an institution for persons with mental illnesses or developmental
29 disabilities or for whom a guardian or conservator has been legally appointed, upon
30 such guardian or conservator, and upon the administrator or superintendent of
31 such institutions for persons with mental illnesses or developmental disabilities, or
32 person having custody, and also upon the person with mental illness or a
33 developmental disability; provided that if the person with mental illness or a
34 developmental disability is a patient of an institution for persons with mental
35 illnesses or developmental disabilities, and the administrator or superintendent

1 thereof shall certify in writing that service upon such person personally would be
2 unavailing or injurious to his physical or mental well-being, and such certificate be
3 filed, service upon such individual may be dispensed with by order of court;
4 (5) If the action is against the state or any of its institutions, departments, or agencies,
5 by service upon such officer or employee as may be designated by the statute
6 authorizing such action, and upon the attorney general. In all matters involving
7 title to land owned or held in trust by the state or any of its institutions,
8 departments, or agencies, upon the commissioner of school and public lands and
9 the attorney general. In all matters other than those involving title to such lands,
10 if no officer or employee is designated, then upon the Governor and the attorney
11 general. Any of such officers or employees referred to in § 15-6-4 may admit
12 service of the summons with the same legal effect as if it had been personally
13 served upon them by an officer or elector;
14 (6) If the action is against a state officer, employee or agent arising out of his office,
15 employment or agency, a copy of the summons and complaint shall be mailed,
16 certified mail, postage prepaid to the attorney general together with an admission
17 of service and a return envelope, postage prepaid, addressed to the sender. The
18 executed admission of service shall be filed by the sender in accordance with § 15-
19 6-5(d);
20 (7) Whenever the manner of service of process is specified in any statute or rule
21 relating to any action, remedy or special proceedings the manner of service so
22 specified shall be followed;
23 (8) In all other cases, to the defendant personally; and
24 (9) If the action is against a person or business entity in a foreign country, service may
25 be made as follows:
26 (i) By an internationally agreed means reasonably calculated to give notice,
27 such as those means authorized by the Hague Convention on the service
28 abroad of judicial and extrajudicial documents; or
29 (ii) If there is no internationally agreed means of service, service reasonably
30 calculated to give notice may be made:
31 (A) In the manner prescribed by the law of the foreign country for
32 service in that country in an action in any of its courts of general
33 jurisdiction;
34 (B) As directed by the foreign authority in response to a letter rogatory
35 or letter request; or
