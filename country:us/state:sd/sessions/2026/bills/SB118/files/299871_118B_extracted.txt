Section 1:
118B 101st Legislative Session 118
2026 South Dakota Legislature
AMENDME NT 118B
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: Senator Crabtree
1 An Act to deposit certain tax revenues into a homeowner tax reduction fund.
2 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
3 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 10-13:
4 There is created in the state treasury the homeowner tax reduction fund. On or
5 before January thirty-first of each year, the treasurer shall deposit in the homeowner tax
6 reduction fund, as created in Senate Bill 125, and enacted by the One Hundred First
7 Legislature, the greater of one hundred million dollars, or the applicable percent of taxes
8 collected in the previous year pursuant to:
9 (1) Chapter 10-45;
10 (2) Chapter 10-46;
11 (3) Chapter 10-46E; and
12 (4) Chapter 10-58; and
13 (5) § 32-5B-20.
14 The Department of Revenue shall administer the fund. The purpose of the fund is
15 to provide a property tax rebate for owner-occupied single-family dwellings. Interest on
16 moneys credited to the fund must remain in the fund. Expenditures from the fund must
17 be budgeted through the general appropriation bill.
18 Moneys in the fund may not be transferred to the general fund.If Senate Bill 125
19 does not become effective, the state treasurer may not transfer moneys under this
20 section.
21 For purposes of this section, "applicable percent" means three-tenths percent
22 divided by the tax rate provided for the taxes referenced in subdivisions (1) to (5),
23 inclusive, of this section, as applicable.
24 Section 2. That § 10-46-48 be AMENDED:
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.
118B 2 118
1 10-46-48. All revenue arising under the operation of this chapter shall
2 immediately be turned over to the state treasurer and by him credited to the general fund,
3 except as otherwise provided in section 1 of this Act.
4 Section 3. That § 10-46E-9 be AMENDED:
5 10-46E-9. The revenue from the tax imposed by this chapter shall be deposited
6 in the general fund, except as otherwise provided in section 1 of this Act.
7 Section 4. That § 10-58-5 be AMENDED:
8 10-58-5. The tax generated by this chapter must be deposited in the general fund,
9 except as otherwise provided in section 1 of this Act.
10 Section 5. This Act is effective beginning July 1, 2027.
