49A 101st Legislative Session 49
2026 South Dakota Legislature
AMENDM ENT 49A
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: The Chair of the Committee on Judiciary at the request of the Attorney General
1 An Act to safeguard the integrity, privacy, and security of genetic data and provide
2 a civil penalty therefor.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 37-24:
5 Terms used in this Act mean:
6 (1) "Biological sample," any part of a human that is known to contain deoxyribonucleic
7 acid;
8 (2) "Consumer," an individual who is a resident of this state;
9 (3) "De-identified data," genetic data that cannot reasonably be used to infer
10 information about, or otherwise be linked to, an identifiable consumer;
11 (4) "Direct-to-consumer genetic testing company," an entity that:
12 (a) Offers genetic testing products or services directly to consumers; or
13 (b) Analyzes, collects, or uses genetic data collected via a direct-to-consumer
14 genetic testing product or service that is provided to the company by the
15 consumer;
16 (5) "Express consent," an affirmative written response, which may be presented and
17 captured electronically;
18 (6) "Genetic data," data other than de-identified data, regardless of format, which
19 concerns a consumer's genetic characteristics; and
20 (7) "Service provider," a person that:
21 (a) Is involved in the collection, transportation, or analysis of, or any other
22 service in connection with, a consumer's biological sample or genetic data,
23 on behalf of a direct-to-consumer genetic testing company;
24 (b) Collects, uses, maintains, or discloses biological samples or genetic data,
25 collected or derived from a direct-to-consumer genetic testing product or
26 service, or directly provided by the consumer; or
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.
49A 2 49
1 (c) Delivers the results of the analysis of a biological sample or genetic data.
2 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 37-24:
3 To safeguard the confidentiality, integrity, privacy, and security of a consumer's
4 genetic data, a direct-to-consumer genetic testing company shall:
5 (1) Make available to the consumer in plain language:
6 (a) A privacy policy that includes basic, essential information about the
7 company's collection, disclosure, and use of genetic data; and
8 (b) A prominent, publicly available privacy notice that includes information
9 about the company's access, consent, data collection, deletion, disclosure,
10 maintenance, retention, security, and transfer practices; and how the
11 company uses genetic data;
12 (2) Provide a clear and complete notice to the consumer that the consumer's de-
13 identified data may be shared with or disclosed to a third party for research
14 purposes, in accordance with 45 C.F.R. part 46 (November 25, 2025);
15 (3) Obtain the consumer's express consent to collect, disclose, or use the consumer's
16 genetic data, including:
17 (a) Initial express consent that describes the uses of genetic data collected
18 through a genetic testing product or service and specifies who has access
19 to the test results and how the genetic data may be shared;
20 (b) Separate express consent, which must include the name of the person
21 receiving the information, for each transfer or disclosure of the consumer's
22 genetic data or biological sample to any person other than the company's
23 vendors and service providers;
24 (c) Separate express consent for each use of the consumer's genetic data or
25 the biological sample beyond the primary purpose of the genetic testing
26 product or service;
27 (d) Separate express consent to retain any biological sample provided by the
28 consumer following completion of the initial testing service requested by the
29 consumer;
30 (e) Informed consent, in compliance with federal policy for the protection of
31 human research subjects under 45 C.F.R. part 46 (November 25, 2025), to
32 transfer or disclose the consumer's genetic data to a third-party for research
33 purposes, or for research conducted under the control of the company for
34 publication or generalizable knowledge purposes; and
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.
49A 3 49
1 (f) Separate express consent for marketing by the direct-to-consumer genetic
2 testing company, to another consumer, based on the consumer's genetic
3 data, or by a third party, to another consumer, based on the consumer
4 having ordered or purchased a genetic testing product or service;
5 (4) Develop, implement, and maintain a security program to protect the consumer's
6 genetic data against unauthorized access, disclosure, or use;
7 (5) Provide a process for the consumer to:
8 (a) Access the consumer's genetic data;
9 (b) Delete the consumer's account and genetic data; and
10 (c) Request and obtain the destruction of the consumer's biological sample;
11 and
12 (6) Provide mechanisms, without any unnecessary steps, for the consumer to revoke
13 any consent of the consumer. At least one mechanism must utilize the primary
14 medium through which the company communicates to the consumer.
15 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 37-24:
16 If a consumer revokes consent pursuant to section 2 of this Act, the company must
17 honor the consumer's revocation of consent within thirty days. If a consumer revokes
18 consent to store the consumer's biological sample, the company must destroy the
19 consumer's biological sample within thirty days of receiving the consumer's revocation of
20 consent.
21 Section 4. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 37-24:
22 A service provider under contract with a direct-to-consumer genetic testing
23 company is subject to the same confidentiality obligations as the direct-to-consumer
24 genetic testing company, as set forth in section 2 of this Act, with respect to all biological
25 samples, genetic data, and information regarding the identity of any consumer that is in
26 the service provider's possession.
27 Section 5. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 37-24:
28 The attorney general may impose a civil penalty against a person for violating
29 section 2 of this Act, section 3 of this Act, or section 4 of this Act. The amount of the civil
30 penalty may not exceed five thousand dollars per violation.
31 Section 6. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 37-24:
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.

1 The provisions of sections 2 to 5, inclusive, of this Act do not apply to:
2 (1) Protected health information collected by a covered entity or business associate,
3 as those terms are defined in 45 C.F.R. § 160.103 (November 25, 2025);
4 (2) A biological sample that is obtained or genetic data that is generated for the
5 purpose of a consumer's medical screening, diagnosis, or treatment;
6 (3) A public or private institution of higher education;
7 (4) An entity owned or operated by a public or private institution of higher education;
8 (5) A forensic laboratory that is operated by, associated with, or under contract with,
9 a law enforcement agency, when performing forensic analysis or related services
10 as part of a criminal investigation; or
11 (6) An entity that analyzes, collects, or uses genetic data or biological samples only in
12 the context of research, as defined in 24 C.F.R. § 164.501 (November 25, 2025),
13 in a manner that complies with the federal policy of the protection of human
14 research subjects under 45 C.F.R. part 46 (November 25, 2025); the Guideline for
15 Good Clinical Practice issued by the International Council for Harmonisation
16 (January 6, 2025); or the United States Food and Drug Administration policy for
17 the protection of human subjects under 21 C.F.R. part 50 (December 4, 2025) and
18 21 C.F.R. part 56 (December 4, 2025); or
19 (7) A hospital licensed under chapter 34-12, including any laboratory or health care
20 facility owned, operated by, or affiliated with the hospital.
