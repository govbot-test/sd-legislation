Introduced by: Representative Schwans
1 An Act to require that the Cosmetology Commission credit work experience for
2 educational hours for certain out-of-state licensure applicants and to declare
3 an emergency.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
5 Section 1. That § 36-15-13 be AMENDED:
6 36-15-13. The commission may shall promulgate rules, in accordance with
7 chapter 1-26, pertaining to the following:
8 (1) Application requirements for any license or permit, as provided for in this chapter;
9 (2) Examination and associated fees;
10 (3) Reports of a student's education and work performed;
11 (4) Minimum standards and requirements for a cosmetology, nail technology, and
12 esthetics salon and school;
13 (5) Minimum standards for plumbing, electrical, physical, and sanitary conditions for
14 the health and safety of an individual utilizing a cosmetology, nail technology, or
15 esthetics salon or school;
16 (6) The professional conduct of a licensee;
17 (7) The reinstatement of a lapsed license and lapsed renewal, as provided for in § 36-
18 15-20.1;
19 (8) The fee for a lapsed license and lapsed renewal, as provided for in § 36-15-20.1;
20 (9) The course and education requirements received by a transfer student, as provided
21 for in § 36-15-34;
22 (10) The textbooks, educational material, and the general course of study for a school
23 and for a cosmetology salon and nail salon having an apprentice, as provided for
24 in § 36-15-46;
25 (11) The progress and education received by an apprentice, as provided for in § 36-15-
26 48;

1 (12) The fees for all inspections, licenses, permits, and renewals;
2 (13) Minimum standards for the use of chemicals or any mechanical or electrical
3 apparatus or appliance;
4 (14) Amount of work experience of an out-of-state licensed applicant that may be used
5 as a substitute for the education requirement, as provided for in §§ 36-15-2.3, 36-
6 15-17, and 36-15-17.1;
7 (15) Inspection and reinspection of an individual licensee, salon, and school;
8 (16)(15) Inspection requested by a prospective salon or booth;
9 (17)(16) Administration and duties of the commission; and
10 (18)(17) Minimum hours of education for esthetics licensing.
11 Section 2. That § 36-15-19.2 be AMENDED:
12 36-15-19.2. The commission may waive the examination required in § 36-15-
13 19.1, 36-15-19.3, or in 36-15-19.5 upon for an individual who holds a license to engage
14 in the practices of cosmetology, from another state, district, territory, or foreign country,
15 if:
16 (1) The individual:
17 (a) Submits an application made to the commission that the applicant
18 possesses, accompanied by the fee provided for in this chapter;
19 (b) Possesses the qualifications required pursuant to § 36-15-15, and that:
20 (1) The applicant holds a currently valid license issued by another state, district,
21 or foreign country and the;
22 (c) Has passed an examination acceptable to the commission in cosmetology,
23 nail technology, or esthetics, as applicable; and
24 (d) Has passed a written examination, prescribed by the commission, on the
25 provisions of this chapter and rules adopted pursuant to this chapter; and
26 (2) The requirements of that state, district, or foreign country, at the time of the
27 examination required by subsection (c) of subdivision (1), were equal to or not less
28 than those required by this state;
29 (2) The applicant has passed an examination acceptable to the commission in
30 cosmetology, nail technology, or esthetics, as applicable;
31 (3) The applicant shall take the prescribed written examination on the provisions of
32 this chapter and rules adopted pursuant to this chapter. The commission shall
33 determine the contents of the examination; and

1194B 3 1194
1 (4) The application is accompanied by the fee provided for in this chapter, except as
2 provided in section 3 of this Act.
3 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 36-15:
4 If an applicant licensed by another state, district, territory, or foreign country holds
5 a cosmetology-related license that is not equivalent to a cosmetology, nail technology, or
6 esthetics license in this state, the commission must allow credit for relevant work
7 experience to apply toward the hours of education required for the cosmetology, nail
8 technician, or esthetics license, as applicable.
9 The commission shall credit two hours of work experience in the applicable field as
10 one hour of school education. Only work experience that occurred after the applicant was
11 initially licensed may be credited. The work experience hours may not be credited for more
12 than half of the hours of education required for the license.
13 The applicant shall submit documentation to the commission, in the form and
14 manner required by the commission, demonstrating the relevant work experience. The
15 commission may deny the issuance of a license, regardless of the amount of work
16 experience submitted by the applicant, if the commission determines that the applicant is
17 not competent for the applicable license.
18 If the applicant obtained the work experience more than five years before the date
19 of application, the commission may require the applicant to take a commission-approved
20 safety and infection control procedures course.
21 Section 4. Whereas, this Act is necessary for the immediate preservation of the public peace,
22 health, or safety, an emergency is hereby declared to exist, and this Act shall be in full force
23 and effect from and after its passage and approval.
