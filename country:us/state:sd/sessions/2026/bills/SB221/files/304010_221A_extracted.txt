FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: Senator Karr
This bill has been extensively amended (hoghoused) and may no longer be consistent with the original
intention of the sponsor.
1 An Act to establish requirements for the distribution and regulate the retail sale of
2 vapor nicotine products, and to provide a penalty therefor.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That § 10-1-28.2 be AMENDED:
5 10-1-28.2. All lists of taxpayers, licensees, or applicants compiled by the
6 Department of Revenue are confidential, except licensees which were licensed under the
7 provisions of chapter 10-47B, 32-6B, 32-6C, 32-7A, or 32-7B, or sections 2 to 21,
8 inclusive, of this Act. It is a Class 2 misdemeanor to disclose any such list except to the
9 extent necessary to carry out the official duties of the department.
10 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 10:
11 Terms used in this chapter mean:
12 (1) "Licensee," a person licensed under the provisions of this chapter;
13 (2) "Nicotine product," a product intended for human consumption, containing any
14 form of the chemical nicotine, including any salt or complex, from any source,
15 nicotinic alkaloids, nicotine analogs, and any substance intended to mimic or
16 replicate the pharmacological effect of nicotine. The term includes delivery
17 systems, components, parts, or accessories. The term does not include food, drugs,
18 devices, or combination products, as defined in the Federal Food, Drug, and
19 Cosmetic Act, 21 U.S.C § 301 et seq. (January 1, 2026), approved for sale by the
20 United States Food and Drug Administration; and
21 (3) "Retailer," a person engaged in the business of selling a nicotine product to an
22 individual for any purpose other than resale.
23 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 10:

1 Any communication by, or on behalf of, the manufacturer distributor, or retailer of
2 a tobacco product that indicates that the product contains a nicotine analog constitutes
3 presumptive evidence that the product contains a nicotine analog.
4 Section 4. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 10:
5 A retailer shall secure a license under this chapter to sell a nicotine product in this
6 state. A separate application and a separate license are required for each place of business
7 of a retailer where a nicotine product is sold at retail.
8 An application for a license required under this section must be made to the
9 Department of Revenue, on forms prescribed by the secretary. The application must
10 contain:
11 (1) The contact information of the applicant;
12 (2) The name and mailing address of the registered agent of the applicant;
13 (3) The physical address and mailing address of the place of business of the retailer;
14 and
15 (4) All past and current violations of state or federal law by the applicant relating to
16 the sale, possession, or transportation of a nicotine product.
17 For retailers where at least fifty percent of the annual gross income in a year for
18 the place of business is estimated to be generated from the sales of nicotine products, the
19 application must be accompanied by a fee of five hundred dollars.
20 For retailers where less than fifty percent of the annual gross income in a year for
21 the place of business is estimated to be generated from the sales of nicotine products, the
22 application must be accompanied by a fee of two hundred fifty dollars.
23 Section 5. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 10:
24 A license issued under section 4 of this Act is valid through December thirtieth
25 following the issuance of the license, unless the license is revoked sooner by the secretary
26 pursuant to this chapter. A license is valid for an additional three days if the application
27 to renew a license is in the possession of the department before midnight on the date of
28 expiration.
29 A license issued under section 4 of this Act may be transferred if the new licensee
30 obtains written approval from the department and is eligible for a license under this
31 chapter.
32 Section 6. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 10:

1 A licensee may apply to the Department of Revenue for a renewal of the license
2 within:

4 (2) Three days after the expiration of the license.

6 the place of business is generated from the sales of nicotine products, the renewal
7 application must be accompanied by a fee of five hundred dollars.
8 For retailers where less than fifty percent of the annual gross income in a year for
9 the place of business is generated from the sales of nicotine products, the renewal
10 application must be accompanied by a fee of two hundred fifty dollars.

12 The Department of Revenue shall deny an application made under section 4 or 6
13 of this Act if:
14 (1) The application includes any false information;
15 (2) The applicant is under the age of twenty-one years; or
16 (3) The applicant has violated any state or federal law relating to the sale, possession,
17 or transportation of a nicotine product.
18 Section 8. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 10:
19 The place of business of a licensee where a nicotine product is sold may not be
20 located within five hundred feet of an elementary or secondary school, unless nicotine
21 products were sold at the place of business prior to July 1, 2026.
22 The distance measured for purposes of this section is from the nearest point of the
23 property on which the place of business is situated, to the nearest point of the property
24 on which the school is situated.

27 (1) Ship, or cause to be shipped, a nicotine product to an individual to be consumed
28 by the individual;
29 (2) Sell, or offer to sell, a nicotine product to a person under the age of twenty-one
30 years;
31 (3) Sell, or offer to sell, a nicotine product in a vending machine;

1 (4) Sell a nicotine product by phone, voice transmission, mail, internet, electronic-
2 application-based service, curbside pick-up, or by any third-party means;
3 (5) Provide free samples of nicotine products to an individual;
4 (6) Purchase a nicotine product from a person that is not a licensed distributor or
5 licensed wholesaler under chapter 10-50 or under the provisions of House Bill 1220,
6 as enacted by the One Hundred First Legislature; or
7 (7) Market, advertise, sell, or cause to be sold a nicotine product where the container
8 or packaging of the nicotine product contains, includes, or is capable of running or
9 interfacing with video games, mobile games, or interactive software on an
10 integrated electronic screen, digital interface, or external gaming system, or is
11 marketed as having gaming functionality.
12 A licensee is responsible for, and may be subject to enforcement action for, any
13 violation of this chapter committed by the licensee's employee, agency, agent, or clerk,
14 while acting within the scope of employment or agency.
15 Section 10. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 10:
16 A licensee shall post a sign, within plain view of any area on the premises where
17 an individual may purchase nicotine products, stating in text, at least one inch in height,
18 that it is illegal to sell nicotine products to any individual under the age of twenty-one.
19 Adjacent to the sign, the licensee shall display a valid license issued under this
20 chapter.
21 Section 11. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 10:
22 A licensee shall keep complete records of all nicotine products held in the licensee's
23 inventory and all nicotine products sold by the licensee, in a manner set forth in rules
24 promulgated by the secretary of the Department of Revenue pursuant to chapter 1-26.
25 The records must include information detailing the name, source, origin,
26 manufacturer, and address of the person supplying, and a copy of the purchase invoice,
27 of the nicotine products in the licensee's inventory.
28 The licensee shall preserve the records for three years.
29 If the records are not stored at the licensee's place of business, the licensee must,
30 upon request of the secretary, make the records available, in electronic form, within five
31 business days.
