AMENDME NT 1095A
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: Representative Reimer
1 An Act to modify provisions pertaining to the submission of a nominating petition
2 and to declare an emergency.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That § 9-13-7 be AMENDED:
5 9-13-7. No candidate for elective municipal office may be nominated unless the
6 candidate files a nominating petition with the municipal finance officer no later than five
7 p.m. on the Tuesday seventy days before the date of the election. A petition is considered
8 filed if it is mailed by registered mail by five p.m. on the last day to file a petition local
9 time, on the second Tuesday of March, for an election held on the first Tuesday after the
10 first Monday in June, or before five p.m. local time, on the second Tuesday of August for
11 an election held on the first Tuesday after the first Monday in November.
12 A candidate may file a nominating petition by sending the petition to the
13 municipality's finance officer, by registered mail, as defined in § 12-1-3. If a candidate
14 files a nominating petition by registered mail, the petition is considered filed if the petition
15 is received by the finance officer on or before March thirty-first, for an election held on
16 the first Tuesday after the first Monday in June or on or before August thirty-first, for an
17 election held on the first Tuesday after the first Monday in November.
18 The petition must be on the form prescribed by the State Board of Elections and
19 must contain:
20 (1) The name of the candidate;
21 (2) The candidate's residential address;
22 (3) The candidate's mailing address, if applicable; and
23 (4) The office the candidate seeks.
24 If an individual who signs a petition lives within a second-class or third-class
25 municipality, the individual may give the individual's post office box number in lieu of a
26 street address.

1 The finance officer may only accept a nominating petition that is on the prescribed

4 nominating petition contains the minimum number of signatures of registered voters
5 within the municipality or ward, and that the candidate is a registered voter within the

7 nomination.
8 Section 2. That § 9-13-7.1 be AMENDED:
9 9-13-7.1. Any person who has filed a nominating petition pursuant to § 9-13-7
10 may withdraw from the nomination by a written request, signed by the person and
11 properly acknowledged and filed with the finance officer of the municipality. The name of
12 a withdrawn or deceased candidate may not be printed on the ballot if the candidate
13 withdraws or dies no later than five p.m. on the deadline day for filing nominating
14 petitions. If the withdrawal or death of a candidate occurs at any time prior to five p.m.
15 on the deadline day for filing nominating petitions results in there being no contest on the
16 ballot, that ballot need not be voted. In addition, if that contest constitutes the only ballot
17 to be voted upon, then the election shall be canceled by the official in charge of the election
18 and the unopposed candidate shall be issued a certificate of election.
19 Any candidate who has filed a nominating petition pursuant to § 9-13-7 may
20 withdraw from the nomination by making a written request under oath. The candidate
21 shall file the request with the municipal finance officer. The finance officer may not print
22 the name of a candidate if the candidate withdraws or dies before five p.m. local time, on
23 the first Thursday after the deadline for filing a nominating petition.
24 If the death or withdrawal of a candidate at any time before the withdrawal
25 deadline results in the number of nominating petitions filed for the vacant position to be
26 equal to or less than the number of vacancies, the contest may not be placed on the ballot,
27 and the finance officer must issue a certificate of election to the unopposed candidate. If
28 that contest is the only contest to be voted upon, the finance officer must cancel the
29 election.

21 of signatures. Upon verification by the business manager, the filing of the nominating
22 petition constitutes nomination and entitles the candidate to have the candidate's name
23 placed on the ballot for the term the candidate specifies on the petition.
