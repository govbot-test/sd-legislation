Section 1:
1006A 101st Legislative Session 1006
2026 South Dakota Legislature
AMENDME NT 1006A
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: Representatives Mortenson, Baxter, Emery, and Odenbach and Senators Grove and Smith at the
request of the State-Tribal Relations
1 An Act to create a taskforce to study the creation of Indian medicaid managed care
2 entities in the state.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. There is established the Indian Medicaid Managed Care Model taskforce to study
5 the creation of Indian medicaid managed care entities, as defined in 42 U.S.C. § 1396u-2(h),
6 in this state. The taskforce must consist of:
7 (1) A representative from each federally recognized Indian tribe located wholly or
8 partially within the state, appointed by the appropriate tribal council;
9 (2) The secretary of the Department of Social Services, or the secretary's designee;
10 (3) The secretary of the Department of Health, or the secretary's designee;
11 (4) The secretary of the Department of Tribal Relations, or the secretary's designee;
12 (5) Two members of the House of Representatives, appointed by the speaker of the
13 House of Representatives;
14 (6) Two members of the Senate, appointed by the president pro tempore of the
15 Senate;
16 (7) A representative of the United States Bureau of Indian Affairs, appointed by the
17 director of the bureau; and
18 (8) The following members, appointed by the Executive Board of the Legislative
19 Research Council:
20 (a) One member representing a healthcare provider;
21 (b) One member representing a healthcare insurance provider; and
22 (c) One healthcare administrator.
23 Any vacancy on the taskforce must be filled in the same manner as the original
24 appointment.
25 At the initial meeting, the members of the taskforce shall select one from among
26 themselves to serve as the chair and one to serve as the vice chair.
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.
1006A 2 1006
1 The Department of Health Social Services shall provide administrative support to
2 the taskforce.
3 The taskforce shall seek input from state and federal executive branch agencies,
4 representatives of the Indian tribes, representatives of the health care industry, and other
5 relevant stakeholders.
6 The taskforce shall report its findings and recommendations, together with any
7 proposed legislation, to the Governor, on or before December 1, 2028.
8 Section 2. This Act is effective beginning January 1, 2027.
