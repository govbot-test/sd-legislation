1282A 101st Legislative Session 1282
2026 South Dakota Legislature
AMENDME NT 1282A
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: Representative Rice
1 An Act to provide for the admission of certain children younger than five years old
2 to the kindergarten program of a school district.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 13-27:
5 The board of a school district may adopt a policy that allows for the admission of a
6 child younger than five to the district's kindergarten program if the child:
7 (1) Is to turn five after September first and before December October first of the year
8 the child is admitted to the kindergarten program; and
9 (2) Demonstrates the cognitive and social skills necessary to meet the kindergarten
10 standards and progress to first grade the following year.
11 Any policy adopted by the board pursuant to this section must describe the process
12 and the procedures by which the district evaluates a child's cognitive and social skills.
13 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 13-28:
14 The parent or guardian of a child younger than five may apply, pursuant to § 13-
15 28-43, to enroll the child in a school district other than the child's resident district if the
16 child's resident district has not adopted a policy allowing for the admission of children
17 younger than five to the district's kindergarten program pursuant to section 1 of this Act.
18 Section 3. That § 13-28-41 be AMENDED:
19 13-28-41. A school district shall grant a request for a transfer into the district or
20 within the district unless the transfer would result in an inability to provide a quality
21 educational program based on criteria established by the district pursuant to § 13-28-44.
22 In addition, a school district shall grant a request to admit into the district a child
23 who is a resident of another school district and who is excused from attending school in
24 the resident district pursuant to § 13-27-2, or is a child provided with alternative

1 instruction pursuant to § 13-27-3, unless admitting the nonresident child would result in
