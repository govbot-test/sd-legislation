Section 1:
93A 101st Legislative Session 93
2026 South Dakota Legislature
AMENDM ENT 93A
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: Senator Reed
1 An Act to prohibit certain state officers or employees from being employed by an
2 organization that was the recipient of a contract awarded on behalf of a state
3 agency for a period of time.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
5 Section 1. That § 5-18A-17 be AMENDED:
6 5-18A-17. No state officer or employee who approves, awards, or administers a
7 contract on behalf of a state agency, may have an interest in a contract or derive a direct
8 benefit from a contract that is within the scope of the officer's or employee's official duties,
9 nor for a one-year period following the end of their employment or position as a state
10 officer may the officer or employee derive a direct benefit as a result of such contract
11 except as provided in § 5-18A-17.2. In addition, no such officer or employee may enter
12 into any contract, other than a contract of employment, with any state agency for a period
13 of one year following their leaving office or employment except as provided in § 5-18A-
14 17.3.This prohibition includes any state officer or employee who, in his or her official
15 capacity, recommends the approval or award of the contract or who supervises a person
16 who approves, awards, or administers the contract. This prohibition does not include any
17 state officer who serves without compensation or who may be paid per diem pursuant to
18 § 4-7-10.4. If an officer or employee of this state has responsibility, on behalf of a state
19 agency, for approving, awarding, or administering a contract, that officer or employee
20 may not:
21 (1) Except as otherwise provided in 5-18A-17.2:
22 (a) Have an interest in a contract or derive a direct benefit from a contract that
23 is within the scope of the officer's or employee's official duties; or
24 (b) Derive a direct benefit from any contract that the officer or employee
25 approved, awarded, or administered on behalf of a state agency for at least

1 one year following the end of the individual's term of office or employment
2 with the state;
3 (2) Except as otherwise provided in § 5-18A-17.3, enter into any contract with a state
4 agency, other than an employment contract, for at least one year following the end
5 of the individual's term of office or employment with the state; or
6 (3) Be Except as otherwise provided in section 3 of this Act, be employed by any
7 organization that was the beneficiary of a contract approved, awarded, or
8 administered by the officer or employee, for a period of:
9 (a) One year, if the value of the contract was not more than one five million
10 dollars; or
11 (b) Two years, if the value of the contract was more than one five million
12 dollars.
13 Nothing in this section may be construed to apply to an officer or employee who
14 serves without compensation or who is only compensated per diem pursuant to § 4-7-
15 10.4.
16 For purposes of this section, the responsibility for approving, awarding, or
17 administering a contract on behalf of a state agency includes supervising the approval,
18 award, or administration of a contract or recommending the approval or award of a
19 contract.
20 Section 2. That § 5-18A-17.2 be AMENDED:
21 5-18A-17.2. A governing body may authorize an officer or employee whose
22 responsibilities include approving, awarding, or administering a contract on behalf of a
23 state agency or; supervising any employee who has these responsibilities approves,
24 awards, or administers a contract on behalf of a state agency; or recommending to any
25 employee the approval or award of a contract on behalf of a state agency, to be a party
26 to or derive a direct benefit from a contract if:
27 (1) The officer or employee has provided full written disclosure to the governing body;
28 (2) The governing body has reviewed the:
29 (a) The essential terms of the transaction or contract and the state officer's or
30 employee's; and
31 (b) The role of the officer or employee in the contract or transaction; and
32 (3) The transaction and the terms of the contract are fair, reasonable, and not contrary
33 to the public interest.

1 The authorization shall be in writing. If a governing body authorizes an officer or
2 employee to be a party to or derive a direct benefit from a contract, the governing body
3 must provide the authorization in writing. Any authorization given pursuant to this section
4 is a public record. Each authorization shall must be filed with the commissioner of human
5 resources and administration, who. The commissioner shall annually compile the
6 authorizations and present them annually for review by to the Government Operations
7 and Audit Committee for review.
8 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 5-18A:
9 A governing body may authorize a former officer or employee whose
10 responsibilities included approving, awarding, or administering a contract on behalf of a
11 state agency; supervising any employee who approved, awarded, or administered a
12 contract on behalf of the state agency; or recommending to any employee the approval
13 or award of a contract on behalf of a state agency, to be employed by an organization that
14 was the beneficiary of a contract approved, awarded, or administered by the officer or
15 employee, before the time permitted in § 5-18A-17, if:
16 (1) The governing body has reviewed:
17 (a) The essential terms of the transaction or contract; and
18 (b) The role of the officer or employee in the contract or transaction; and
19 (2) The transaction and the terms of the contract are fair, reasonable, and not contrary
20 to the public interest.
21 The governing body must provide the authorization in writing. Any authorization
22 given pursuant to this section is a public record. Each authorization must be filed with the
23 commissioner of human resources and administration. The commissioner shall annually
24 compile the authorizations and present them to the Government Operations and Audit
25 Committee for review.
