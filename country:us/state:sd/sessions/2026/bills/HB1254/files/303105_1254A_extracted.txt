Section 1:
1254A 101st Legislative Session 1254
2026 South Dakota Legislature
AMENDME NT 1254A
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: Representative Heinemann
1 An Act to exempt any soil amendment used exclusively for agricultural purposes
2 from sales tax, and to modify the definition of a soil amendment.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 10-45:
5 There are specifically exempted from the provisions of this chapter and from the
6 computation of the amount of tax imposed by it, the gross receipts from the sale of any
7 soil amendment, as defined in § 38-19A-1, provided five hundred pounds or more of any
8 one of these products is sold in a single sale to be used exclusively for agricultural
9 purposes.
10 Section 2. That § 38-19A-1 be AMENDED:
11 38-19A-1. Terms as used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise requires,
12 shall mean:
13 (1) "Brand," any trademark, product name, or other specific designation under which
14 any individual soil amendment is offered for sale;
15 (2) "Bulk," nonpackaged form;
16 (3) "Distributing," importing, consigning, manufacturing, producing, compounding,
17 mixing or blending any soil amendment, or offering for sale, selling, bartering, or
18 otherwise supplying any soil amendment in this state;
19 (4) "Distributor," any person who distributes any soil amendment in this state;
20 (5) "Investigational allowance," any allowance for variations inherent in the taking,
21 preparation and analysis of an official sample of a soil amendment;
22 (6) "Label," any display of all written, printed, or graphic matter upon the immediate
23 container or statement accompanying a soil amendment;
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.
1254A 2 1254
1 (7) "Labeling," any written, printed, or graphic matter, upon or accompanying any soil
2 amendment, or advertisements, brochures, posters, or television or radio
3 announcements used in promoting the sale of such soil amendment;
4 (7A) "Manipulated manure," any animal or vegetable manure collected or stored in a
5 manner consistent with practices commonly implemented in agricultural production
6 that has been subjected to practices including composting, mechanical dewatering,
7 or pelletizing or altered in any way to change chemical, physical, or biological
8 characteristics;
9 (7B) "Microbe," any microbiological organism or mixture of microbiological organisms
10 intended to produce any physical, chemical, biochemical, biological, or other
11 change in the soil;
12 (8) "Minimum percentage," that percentage of soil amendment ingredient that shall be
13 present in a product before the product may be accepted for registration when
14 distributed in any form or manner;
15 (9) "Official sample," any sample of a soil amendment taken by the secretary and so
16 designated;
17 (10) "Percent," percentage by weight;
18 (11) "Registrant," any person who shall register any soil amendments under the
19 provisions of this chapter;
20 (12) "Secretary," the secretary of the Department of Agriculture and Natural Resources
21 of the State of South Dakota;
22 (13) "Soil amending ingredient," any substance which will improve the physical,
23 chemical, or other characteristic of the soil or improve crop production;
24 (14) "Soil amendment," any substance which is intended to improve the physical,
25 chemical, or other characteristics of the soil or improve crop production, except
26 the following: commercial fertilizer, unmanipulated animal manures,
27 unmanipulated vegetable manures, pesticides, lime or lime sludge produced by a
28 water treatment facility, sewage sludge, as defined in § 38-19-1, and compost as
29 defined in § 38-19-1;
30 (15) "Soil ingredient form," any ingredient or the chemical compound of an ingredient;
31 (16) "Ton," two thousand pounds avoirdupois net weight; and
32 (17) "Weight," the weight of that material offered for sale.
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.
