10A 101st Legislative Session 10
2026 South Dakota Legislature
AMENDM ENT 10A
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: The Chair of the Committee on Transportation at the request of the Department of Public Safety
1 An Act to revise a provision related to the adoption of federal motor carrier
2 regulations.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That § 49-28A-3 be AMENDED:
5 49-28A-3. The state hereby adopts Title 49 of the Code of Federal Regulations,
6 subtitle B, chapter I, subchapter A, part 107 (subparts F and G only) and subchapter C,
7 parts 171 to 180, inclusive, as amended through January 1, 2025 2026, and Title 49 of
8 the Code of Federal Regulations, subtitle B, chapter III, subchapter B, part 387 and parts
9 390 to 397, inclusive, as amended through January 1, 2025 2026, with the following
10 modifications:
11 (1) All references to interstate operations must also include intrastate operations
12 except that drivers and motor carriers operating intrastate vehicles and
13 combinations of vehicles with two axles or less or with a gross vehicle weight rating
14 of not more than twenty-six thousand pounds that with a gross vehicle weight
15 rating, gross combination weight rating, gross vehicle weight, or gross combination
16 weight that does not equal or exceed twenty six thousand one pounds, which are
17 not used to transport hazardous materials requiring placarding under part 177, or
18 designed to transport more than fifteen passengers, including the driver, are not
19 subject to parts 390 to 397, inclusive;
20 (2) For the purposes of 49 C.F.R. § 391.11(b)(1), a driver must be at least twenty-one
21 years old if engaged in interstate commerce, or transporting hazardous material of
22 a type or quantity requiring placarding under part 177, or operating a vehicle
23 designed to transport more than fifteen passengers, including the driver. All other
24 drivers must be at least eighteen years old;
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.

1 (3) Unless required by an employer to be medically certified under Title 49 of the Code
2 of Federal Regulations, intrastate drivers are exempt from the physical
3 requirements of 49 C.F.R. § 391.41.
4 Any violation of part 387 and parts 390 to 396, inclusive, the motor carrier safety
5 requirements governing the qualifications of drivers, driving of motor vehicles, parts and
6 accessories necessary for safe operation, notification and reporting of accidents,
7 assistance with investigations and special studies, hours of service of drivers, inspection,
8 repair, and maintenance, found in part 387 and parts 390 to 396, inclusive, is a Class 2
9 misdemeanor.
10 Any violation of the hazardous materials regulations pertaining to registration of
11 cargo tank motor vehicles, registration of persons who offer or transport hazardous
12 materials, general information, regulations and definitions, hazardous materials tables,
13 hazardous materials communication regulations, and test and inspection marking
14 requirements, found in parts 107 (subparts F and G only), 171, 172, and 178 to 180,
15 inclusive, is a Class 2 misdemeanor.
16 Any violation of the hazardous materials regulations pertaining to packaging,
17 prohibited shipments, loading and unloading, segregation and separation, retesting and
18 inspection of cargo tanks, and other carriage by regulations found in parts 173 to 180,
19 inclusive, or violation of the driving and parking rules, found in part 397, is a Class 1
20 misdemeanor.
