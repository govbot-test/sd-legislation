Section 1:
1061A 101st Legislative Session 1061
2026 South Dakota Legislature
AMENDME NT 1061A
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: Representative Sjaarda
1 An Act to require the acceptance of cash as payment in certain transactions.
2 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
3 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 37-24:
4 Except as provided in section 2 of this Act, a person must accept cash, at least at
5 one point-of-sale, as a form of payment for any goods or services sold at retail to a
6 purchaser provided:
7 (1) The sale and payment for the goods or services occurs in person;
8 (2) The person selling the goods or services, or an individual employed by the person
9 selling the goods or services, is physically present at the time of the sale;
10 (3) The purchaser of the goods or services chooses to pay the purchase price using
11 cash; and
12 (4) The consideration total payment received by the person selling the goods or
13 services in the transaction is one hundred dollars or less.
14 A person selling goods or services may not charge a consumer a higher price for
15 the use of cash as a form of payment.
16 For purposes of this section, "cash" means physical coin, currency, or notes that
17 are designated as legal tender by federal law.
18 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 37-24:
19 The provisions of section 1 of this Act do not apply to:
20 (1) Federally chartered credit unions;
21 (2) Financial institutions, as defined in § 10-43-1;
22 (3) The sale of goods or services that require a credit card for purposes of placing a
23 security deposit or covering unforeseen damages or expenses; or
24 (4) The sale of, or payment for, goods or services occurring on an airplane; or
25 (5) A transaction for a rental agreement of a rental car, as defined by § 58-30-197.
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.

1 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 37-24:
2 The state and, its political subdivisions, and any private person or business acting
3 as an agent of the state or a political subdivision, must accept cash as a form of payment
4 for any fee or charge imposed on any member of the public if:
5 (1) The payment is submitted to the correct office in person;
6 (2) The member of the public chooses to submit payment in the form of cash; and
7 (3) The total payment received by the state or political subdivision is one hundred
8 dollars or less.
9 The state and its political subdivisions may not impose a higher fee or charge upon
10 any member of the public for the use of cash as a form of payment.
11 For purposes of this section, "cash" means physical coin, currency, or notes that
12 are designed as legal tender by federal law.
