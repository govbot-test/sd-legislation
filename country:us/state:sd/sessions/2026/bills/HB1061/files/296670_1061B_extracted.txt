1061B 101st Legislative Session 1061
2026 South Dakota Legislature
AMENDME NT 1061B
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: Representative Sjaarda
1 An Act to require the acceptance of cash as payment in certain transactions.
2 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
3 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 37-24:
4 Except as provided in section 2 of this Act, a person must accept cash as a form of
5 payment for any goods or services sold at retail to a purchaser provided:
6 (1) The sale and payment for the goods or services occurs in person;
7 (2) The person selling the goods or services, or an individual employed by the person
8 selling the goods or services, is physically present at the time of the sale;
9 (3) The purchaser of the goods or services chooses to pay the purchase price using
10 cash; and
11 (4) The consideration received by the person selling the goods or services in the
12 transaction is one hundred dollars or less.
13 A person selling goods or services may not charge a consumer a higher price for
14 the use of cash as a form of payment.
15 For purposes of this section, "cash" means physical coin, currency, or notes that
16 are designated as legal tender by federal law.
17 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 37-24:
18 The provisions of section 1 of this Act do not apply to:
19 (1) Federally chartered credit unions;
20 (2) Financial institutions, as defined in § 10-43-1;
21 (3) The sale of goods or services that require a credit card for purposes of placing a
22 security deposit or covering unforeseen damages or expenses; or
23 (4) The sale of, or payment for, goods or services occurring on an airplane.
24 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 37-24:
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.
1061B 2 1061
1 The state and its political subdivisions must accept cash as a form of payment for
2 any fee or charge imposed on any member of the public if:
3 (1) The payment is submitted to the correct office in person;
4 (2) The member of the public chooses to submit payment in the form of cash; and
5 (3) The total payment received by the state or political subdivision is one hundred
6 dollars or less.
7 The state and its political subdivisions may not impose a higher fee or charge upon
8 any member of the public for the use of cash as a form of payment.
9 For purposes of this section, "cash" means physical coin, currency, or notes that
10 are designed as legal tender by federal law.
11 Section 4. This Act is effective beginning July 1, 2027.
