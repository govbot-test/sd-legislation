Section 1:
1173C 101st Legislative Session 1173
2026 South Dakota Legislature
AMENDME NT 1173C
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
On the Introduced bill, delete the previously adopted amendment (1173A), to read as follows:
Introduced by: Representative Hughes
1 An Act to require an environmental impact statement when certain applicants that
2 seek a permit from the Public Utilities Commission of the State of South
3 Dakota to prepare and pay for an environmental impact statement.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
5 Section 1. That § 49-41B-12 be AMENDED:
6 49-41B-12. At the time of filing an application as required in § 49-41B-11, an
7 applicant must deposit the minimum fee with the commission. If required by the
8 commission, an applicant must remit an amount to be determined by the commission
9 based upon the actual cost of investigating, reviewing, processing, and serving notice of
10 an application. The amount must be deposited with the state treasurer and credited to a
11 subfund within the designated revenue regulatory assessment fee fund and may only be
12 disbursed on vouchers approved by the commission for the actual cost of investigating,
13 reviewing, processing, and serving notice of the application. Expenditures from the
14 revenue fund must be budgeted in the general appropriation bill. Except as otherwise
15 provided in this section or agreed to by an applicant, the maximum fee chargeable may
16 not exceed one-quarter of one percent of the first one hundred million dollars of estimated
17 construction cost plus one-twentieth of one percent of all additional estimated construction
18 costs of the facility. To exceed the maximum fee when the applicant has not agreed to a
19 fee higher than the maximum amount, the commission must make a finding upon a motion
20 from the commission staff that all costs incurred were reasonably necessary to investigate,
21 review, process, and serve notice of the application. The minimum total fee chargeable
22 may not be less than twenty thousand dollars. The minimum fee is nonrefundable unless
23 ordered by the commission.
24 Moneys from the South Dakota public utilities commission gross receipts tax fund,
25 as established in § 49-1A-2, may not be used to reimburse the commission for any
26 additional costs incurred.
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.
