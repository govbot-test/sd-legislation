Section 1:
1112A 101st Legislative Session 1112
2026 South Dakota Legislature
AMENDME NT 1112A
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: Representative Mulally
1 An Act to modify the definition of an electronic smoking device.
2 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
3 Section 1. That § 34-46-1 be AMENDED:
4 34-46-1. Terms used in this chapter mean:
5 (1) "Electronic smoking device," any e-cigarette, e-cigar, e-pipe, e-hookah, or vape
6 pen, containing or delivering nicotine or any other substance with nicotine intended
7 for human consumption that, which may be used by a person in any manner for
8 the purpose of inhaling vapor or aerosol from the product. The term does not
9 include any product approved by the United States Food and Drug Administration
10 for sale as a tobacco cessation product or for any other therapeutic purpose, and
11 is marketed and sold solely for that approved purpose;
12 (2) "Proof of age," a driver's license, nondriver identification card, tribal identification
13 card, or other generally accepted means of identification that contains a picture of
14 the individual and appears on its face to be valid;
15 (3) "Sample," tobacco products distributed to members of the general public at no cost
16 for purposes of promoting the product;
17 (4) "Sampling," the distribution of samples to members of the general public in a public
18 place;
19 (5) "Self-service display," a display that contains cigarettes or smokeless tobacco, or
20 both, and is located in an area openly accessible to the merchant's consumers, and
21 from which such consumers can readily access cigarettes or smokeless tobacco, or
22 both, without the assistance of the merchant or an employee or agent of the
23 merchant. A display case that holds tobacco products behind locked doors does not
24 constitute a self-service display;
25 (6) "Smoke" or "Smoking," the act of inhaling, exhaling, burning, or carrying any
26 lighted or heated cigar, cigarette, pipe, hookah, or any other lighted or heated
Underscores indicate new language.

1 tobacco or plant product intended for inhalation, whether natural or synthetic, in
2 any manner or in any form, including the use of an electronic smoking device which
3 creates an aerosol or vapor, in any manner or in any form;
4 (7) "Tobacco product," any item made of tobacco intended for human consumption,
5 including cigarettes, cigars, pipe tobacco, and smokeless tobacco, and vapor
6 products as defined in § 34-46-20;
7 (8) "Tobacco speciality store," a business that derives at least seventy-five percent of
