Section 1:
222B 101st Legislative Session 222
2026 South Dakota Legislature
AMENDME NT 222B
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: Senator Karr
1 An Act to make an appropriation for eligible water, wastewater, storm water, and
2 nonpoint source projects riparian buffer initiative projects and to declare an
3 emergency.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
5 Section 1. There is appropriated $10,000,000 from the general fund, to the Department of
6 Agriculture and Natural Resources, for the purpose of providing grants for eligible water,
7 wastewater, storm water, and nonpoint source projects in accordance with the guidance
8 provided in section 2 of this Act.
9 Section 2. To be eligible for funding, projects must go through the Department of Agriculture
10 and Natural Resources by way of the Board of Water and Natural Resources and be included
11 in the state water plan. The following funding terms apply to eligible projects under this Act:
12 (1) Eligible applicants may receive a grant award limited to a per person project cost
13 based on the population served by the system or municipality, using the 2020
14 federal census numbers or system service numbers, as determined by the board.
15 The grant limit must apply to each applicant, regardless of the project cost or the
16 number of projects in need of funding, and must be determined as follows:
17 (a) Systems or municipalities with service populations of up to one thousand
18 may receive up to an eighty percent grant, with a maximum project cost
19 for determining the grant amount limited to nine thousand dollars per
20 person per applicant for all eligible projects considered for grants;
21 (b) Systems or municipalities with service populations with at least one
22 thousand and up to two thousand five hundred may receive up to a fifty
23 percent grant with a maximum project cost for determining the grant
24 amount limited to seven thousand dollars per person per applicant for all
25 eligible projects considered for grants; and
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.

1 (c) Systems or municipalities with service populations greater than two
2 thousand five hundred may receive a thirty percent grant with a maximum
3 project cost for determining the grant amount limited to three thousand
4 dollars per person per applicant for all eligible projects considered for
5 grants;
6 (2) Projects addressing regionalization and consolidation, environmental and health
7 regulatory compliance issues, drought resiliency, water availability, nonpoint
8 source improvements, or other significant environmental issues may receive
9 funding above the grant limits in this Act. The department shall evaluate projects
10 and make recommendations to the board for funding based on need and positive
11 long-term benefits of the project; and
12 (3) Eligible engineering studies for projects with long term drought resiliency, drinking
13 water availability issues, or water or wastewater regionalization projects may be
14 awarded a grant in an amount up to one hundred percent of the study cost.
15 The board has discretion to deny funding for lake dredging and projects that do
16 not align with cost effective regionalization.
17 Section 1. There is appropriated $10,000,000 from the general fund, to the Department of
18 Agriculture and Natural Resources, for the purpose of providing grants as provided in section
19 2 of this Action.
20 Section 2. Of the amount appropriated in section 1 of this Act, $2,000,000 is allocated for
21 eligible water, wastewater, and storm water projects. To be eligible for funding from this
22 allocation, projects must be approved by the Board of Water and Natural Resources and be
23 included in the state water plan.
24 Of the amount appropriated in section 1 of this Act, $8,000,000 is allocated for statewide
25 riparian buffer initiative projects.
26 Section 3. The secretary of the Department of Agriculture and Natural Resources shall
27 approve vouchers and the state auditor shall draw warrants to pay expenditures authorized
28 by this Act.
29 Section 4. Any amounts appropriated in this Act not lawfully expended shall revert in
30 accordance with the procedures prescribed in chapter 4-8.

3 full force and effect from and after its passage and approval.
