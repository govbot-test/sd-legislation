1213D 101st Legislative Session 1213
2026 South Dakota Legislature
AMENDME NT 1213D
FOR THE INTRODUCED BILL
Introduced by: Representative Muckey
1 An Act to make an appropriation for a statewide educator retention initiative.
2 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
3 Section 1. There is appropriated $2,500,000 from the general fund, to the Department of
4 Education, for the purpose of providing grants to school districts and accredited nonpublic
5 schools as part of a statewide educator retention initiative.
6 Section 2. The statewide educator retention initiative must employ a comprehensive,
7 educator-centered approach that uses quantitative and qualitative data to address issues that
8 impede a school district's guide and measure the impact of actions taken by a school district
9 or accredited nonpublic school to improve the workplace conditions that have the greatest
10 impact on educator retention. The Department of Education shall administer and make
11 available the moneys appropriated for the statewide educator retention initiative, pursuant to
12 section 1 of this Act, to directly assist to provide grants to school districts and accredited
13 nonpublic schools in resolving issues that impede educator retention. The department may
14 partner with a qualified organization or entity to provide the statewide educator retention
15 initiative that apply to participate in the initiative.
16 Section 3. Any school district or accredited nonpublic school that is the recipient of a grant
17 under section 2 of this Act must partner with an entity that has the ability to provide research-
18 based tools and advisory support to elevate educator voices, reduce educator burnout, and
19 improve the daily working conditions of educators. The Department of Education shall provide
20 a list of entities that meet the requirements of this section to any interested school district or
21 accredited nonpublic school upon request prior to the opening of the application period as
22 provided in this section.
23 Section 4. A school district or accredited nonpublic school may apply to participate in the
24 initiative only during the designated application period beginning September 1, 2026, and
25 ending on October 31, 2026. If the Department of Education does not obligate all of the

1 moneys appropriated in section 1 of this Act, before June 30, 2029, the department must
2 initiate a second application period, beginning on July 1, 2029, and ending on August 31,
3 2029.
4 Section 5. The Department of Education shall provide a written report to the Joint Committee
5 on Appropriations, the House Committee on Education, and the Senate Committee on
6 Education, on or before February first each year. The report must detail the results of the
7 initiative in each school district or accredited nonpublic school that received a grant. Upon the
8 request of the chair of a committee receiving the report, the secretary of the Department of
9 Education shall make a presentation to the committee and answer any questions that the
10 committee may have on the contents of the report.
11 Section 6. The Department of Education shall provide grants for costs associated with
12 participation in the statewide educator retention initiative, based on applications received
13 from school districts and accredited nonpublic schools and approved by the Department of
14 Education during the period beginning on July 1, 2026, and ending on June 30, 2029.The
15 department may spend up to five percent of the appropriation in section 1 of this Act for costs
16 incurred in administering the grants.
17 Section 6. The secretary of the Department of Education shall approve vouchers and the
18 state auditor shall draw warrants to pay expenditures authorized in this Act.
19 Section 7. Any amounts appropriated in this Act not lawfully expended or obligated shall
20 revert in accordance with the procedures prescribed in chapter 4-8.
21 Section 8. This Act is effective beginning June 30, 2026.
